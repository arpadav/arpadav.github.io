    2025 - NvRadar Early Access + Dev

      Pilot program to assess Nvidia's radar processing libraries

      Collaboration between JHU/APL & Nvidia - Status: Complete

      Jul 2025 - Nov 2025 - Columnia, Maryland

    Disclamer: I will keep from disclosing anything proprietary and confidential, since I'm sure this effort is still on-going and certain technologies have yet to be made public.

    Overview

    When I was at JHU/APL, we had the opportunity to work with Nvidia, where we got to work directly together on radar processing algorithms and pipelines.

    What I can share is what has been made public, at a conference I attended at Nvidia's GTC in in Washington DC:

    • General Presentation on NvRadar
    • Our/APL Presentation

    Where in the latter talk, I was responsible for benchmarking APL's capabilities versus Nvidia's, and giving feedback to Nvidia's interface for developers using their SDK. Now, it's reached its way into the official documentation:

    • https://developer.nvidia.com/docs/drive/drive-os/7.0.3/public/drive-os-linux-sdk/embedded-software-components/DRIVE_AGX_SoC/DriveWorks/DriveWorks_SDK/samples/sensors/plugins/radar/index.html

    Personal Statement

    A lot of my work at JHU/APL involved deep learning for synthetic-aperature-radar, both in a theoretical and an application sense. A lot of really fun research was involved in working with signal-native deep learning architectures, alongside setting up data pipelines for training scenarios, then being assigned to multiple operational environments for testing any and all technologies with the end-users themselves.
